How I Think About Growth

The operating principles
behind every decision

Good growth strategy isn't a collection of tactics. It's a set of beliefs about how acquisition, retention, and revenue actually work - and the discipline to hold them under pressure.

01
Attribution before optimisation.
You cannot optimise what you cannot measure accurately. Before scaling any channel, I ensure the attribution model is clean, the windows are calibrated, and the signals reflect true incrementality - not last-touch flattery. Bad data at the top of the funnel corrupts every downstream decision.
02
Incrementality over vanity metrics.
Reported conversions are not the same as caused conversions. I test for real lift - using ghost bidding, holdout groups, and controlled incrementality studies - because retargeting campaigns that "perform" on paper can be spending against behaviour that would have happened anyway.
03
Scale follows repeatability.
You can't scale chaos. Every channel I build goes through a structured test-learn-codify cycle before budget is committed at scale. That's why I started programmatic with controlled DSP experiments and only pushed spend when the model had proven stability - growing from ₹2.3 Cr to ₹12 Cr on the back of repeatable processes.
04
CAC matters only in relation to LTV.
A ₹3,000 CAC can be excellent or catastrophic depending on the user it brings in. I build acquisition strategy around cohort quality and downstream revenue - which is why I'll willingly accept a higher CAC from non-playable ads when data shows those users deposit more and churn less.
05
Channel diversity is a risk management decision.
Over-reliance on one or two platforms is a strategic liability. When Meta and Google are the only pipes, any policy change, CPM spike, or algorithm shift becomes an existential event. Building programmatic from zero at A23 was as much about resilience as it was about reach.
06
Growth is a systems problem.
Sustainable growth comes from well-designed acquisition systems - with clear feedback loops between channels, creative, data, and product - not from optimising individual campaigns in isolation. I invest in the infrastructure that makes the whole machine smarter over time.

A/B Experiment
Experiment Strategist · Feb 2024

Closing the Deposit Gap: Localised Trust Signals at the Payment Step

A significant number of KYC-verified users were viewing the Add-Cash page but not converting. The hypothesis: this was a trust problem, not a traffic problem. We designed a controlled A/B test (20:80 split) targeting MH users - showing a Marathi testimonial video to users who exited the page, then prompting a return to Add-Cash. Primary metrics were D0 and D0–D7 conversion rates. Secondary metrics tracked retention and revenue quality to ensure the intervention wasn't pulling in low-value users.

Why it mattered: Confirmed the trust hypothesis and unlocked a rollout strategy across KA, TN, and ROI - each with language-matched testimonials - with ROI delivering +2.6% D0–D7 lift.

Program Lead · May – Jul 2023

Playable vs Standard Ads: When Lower CPIs Signal a User Quality Problem

Playable ads were generating strong install efficiency - CPI dropped 52%, CPR dropped 60%. The strategic question wasn't whether playables could bring users cheaply; it was whether those users were worth acquiring. We ran a structured comparison across CPI, CPR, CAC, and ROAS, looking specifically at post-install monetisation behaviour. The result revealed a fundamental tension: the interactivity that drives installs also attracts users who engage with gameplay but have low deposit intent.

Why it mattered: The experiment prevented a budget shift that would have looked efficient on top-funnel metrics while quietly degrading ROAS. Decision: ring-fence playables for awareness objectives; protect deposit-intent budgets with non-playable formats.

Attribution Strategy
Program Lead · Jul 2025

Fixing the Attribution Model: From 30-Day Lag to 7-Day Precision

A 30-day attribution window was creating two compounding problems: retargeting audiences were stale by the time they could be re-activated, and blended CAC was inflated by incorrectly attributed conversions. I led a cross-functional initiative - aligning BI, Analytics, Strategy, and Marketing - to migrate to a 7-day window across Meta, Google, and programmatic. This required building a measurement framework that could distinguish true organic loss from attribution reassignment, and pre-briefing leadership on the expected organic dip before rollout.

Why it mattered: The 8% paid conversion uplift in the first 7 days, combined with faster RT cycles and cleaner reporting, gave the team decision-making clarity it didn't previously have. The 6% organic dip was model correction, not performance loss.

Incrementality Study
Experiment Strategist · May – Jul 2024

Proving Retargeting ROI: Ghost Bidding Incrementality on Repeat Purchase

Retargeting budgets were being renewed on the basis of attributed conversions - but there was no evidence these conversions were caused by the ads rather than by organic user intent. I contributed to designing a ghost bidding incrementality study on mDSP - a 90/10 split where the control group was shown PSAs instead of retargeting creatives. Segmentation was precise: users who had made their first purchase in the last 120 days, excluding anyone who had purchased in the last 7 days. Lift was measured across app opens (re-engagement) and 2nd purchase (monetisation).

Why it mattered: The 16.8% incremental 2UP lift and 22.3% incremental app open uplift gave the business statistically grounded justification to scale retargeting spend - replacing assumption-based budget allocation with proof.
